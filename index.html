Aerodynamics

Aerodynamics in electric race bikes plays a crucial role in enhancing performance, speed, and efficiency. By reducing drag, these bikes can achieve higher speeds and improve overall efficiency, which is particularly important for conserving battery power. Streamlined bodywork with smooth, flowing lines, along with fairings and windshields, minimizes air resistance and turbulence. Aerodynamic wings and spoilers create downforce, enhancing grip and stability at high speeds.

Above is the picture of carbon fibre which is used in carbon lay-up of aero parts. Carbon lay-up for fairings involves layering carbon fiber sheets to create lightweight, strong, and aerodynamic components for electric race bikes. This enhances performance by reducing weight and maintaining structural integrity. The precise fiber placement ensures optimal strength and rigidity, minimizing drag and maximizing efficiency on the racetrack. For Charge Chasers, expertly crafted carbon lay-up fairings are crucial for peak performance in electric super bike racing.

Electric Powertrain

The E-powertrain subsystem in an electric racing bike is responsible for converting stored electrical energy from the battery into mechanical power to drive the bike. It includes components such as the electric motor, motor controller, battery management system (BMS), and transmission. This subsystem ensures efficient energy utilization, optimal power delivery, and seamless integration with other bike systems to maximize performance, speed, and reliability on the racetrack. Its design and optimization are crucial for achieving competitive edge and sustainability in electric racing.

In the Chase Chargers team, we use the Datai motor for its high efficiency and robust performance, ensuring our electric racing bikes achieve superior speed and acceleration. Paired with the Datai motor controller, we gain precise control over power delivery and seamless integration with our battery management system. This combination enhances our bike's overall performance, reliability, and competitiveness on the racetrack.

Mechanical

The mechanical subsystem of the Chase Chargers' electric racing bikes focuses on converting electrical energy into mechanical motion efficiently and reliably. This subsystem includes components such as the frame, suspension, drivetrain, and braking systems, all meticulously engineered to handle the high torque and speed demands of electric propulsion. Advanced materials and precision engineering ensure optimal weight distribution, stability, and durability. The integration of these mechanical elements allows for superior handling and performance on the track, providing a competitive edge in student racing competitions.

The Chase Chargers' custom-made trellis chassis is engineered for exceptional rigidity and lightweight performance. Constructed from high-strength tubular steel, it optimizes weight distribution and enhances stability and handling. This innovative design ensures structural integrity under high racing stresses while allowing easy maintenance and component access. The result is a robust, agile, and highly responsive chassis, contributing to the bike's competitive edge.

Management

The management subsystem of the Chase Chargers' student racing team is pivotal in ensuring smooth operations and optimal performance. It oversees project planning, resource allocation, and team coordination, ensuring that all aspects of bike development are on schedule and within budget. This subsystem also manages communication between different departments, facilitating seamless collaboration and quick problem resolution. Additionally, it monitors progress through regular meetings and reports, ensuring that the team's objectives are consistently met and that the electric racing bikes are built to the highest standards.

In our discussion of various subsystems in management, we explored essential components that drive the Chase Chargers' success. Key subsystems include project planning, which sets timelines and milestones; resource allocation, ensuring materials and finances are effectively distributed; and team coordination, fostering collaboration and efficiency. We also highlighted the importance of communication management for seamless inter-departmental interaction and problem-solving, as well as progress monitoring through regular updates and evaluations to ensure all objectives are met.
